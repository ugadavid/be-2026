When speaking, content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives and adverbs) generally receive more stress than function words (determiners, auxiliary verbs, prepositions). Which of these content words receive the most emphasis will depend on the context and meaning.

Listen to the following audio examples and identify which words (and which syllables within those words) receive more stress. Then repeat the phrases aloud and compare your own voice with the example.

He has blue eyes.

This package is too heavy.

The baby slept soundly through the night.

He spoke softly to the child.

He is currently living in the centre of town, but he would like to move to a rural village.
