When speaking, content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives and adverbs) generally receive more stress than function words (determiners, auxiliary verbs, prepositions). Which of these four types of content word receive the most emphasis will depend upon the context and meaning.

 

Listen to the following audio examples, and identify which words (and which syllables within those words) receive more stress. Then repeat the phrases aloud.

 

 

He has blue eyes.

This package is too heavy.

The baby slept soundly through the night.

He spoke softly to the child.

He is currently living in the centre of town, but he would like to move to a rural village.

 

If in doubt concerning word and syllable stress, check a dictionary. Word stress is usually indicated by an apostrophe (') placed before the stressed syllable. (ex. ‘heavy, inter’national)
